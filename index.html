Exploring Bidibidi Refugee Settlement

Conflict in South Sudan

The Bidi Bidi refugee settlement in northwestern Uganda is home to about 270,000 refugees from South Sudan fleeing a brutal civil war. It only took two years after South Sudan gained independence from Sudan in 2011 for war to breakout amongst the young nation. The civil war began after President Kiir accused Vice President Riek Machar of attempting a coup d’état. The conflict has been a struggle between power for between the ruling political party, the Sudan People’s Liberation Movement (SPLM), and the Machar led party, the Sudan people’s Liberation Movement in Opposition (SLPM-IO). The civil war has been characterized by its brutality between ethnic groups, with events like the 2014 Bentui Massacre where over 400 civilians were killed. The conflict has killed an estimated 400,000 people and forced another 2.5 million people to flee to other countries, including Uganda.
